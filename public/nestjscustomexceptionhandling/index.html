Key Takeaways (What You'll Learn)

  • NestJS has built-in errors, but you can make them better!
  • We'll create custom filters by using the `ExceptionFilter` and `@Catch()` tools.
  • Inside filters, we get error details and can change the response.
  • You can use `HttpAdapterHost` to play with requests and responses directly.
  • Errors can be different for development and when your app is live (production).
  • Making them "global" means they apply everywhere automatically.
  • You can catch ALL errors with a simple `@Catch()` decorator.
  • Creating your own error types (like `InvalidCredentialsException`) is super useful.
