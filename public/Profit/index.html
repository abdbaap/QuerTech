Google AdSense Approval:
PROVEN Guide to Beat Rejection

This video provides a practical, experience-based guide for achieving Google AdSense approval by effectively countering "low-value content" rejections. The core message emphasizes creating long-form, original content rooted in personal expertise, and crucially, applying a distinct "human touch" even when utilizing AI writing tools. Following these specific strategies is presented as a direct path to monetization.

Video Purpose and Speaker Tone

The video's purpose is to guide content creators on how to successfully get their websites approved by Google AdSense, specifically addressing and resolving the common "low-value content" rejection. The speaker maintains an informative, encouraging, and experienced tone, drawing on his personal success of achieving first-attempt AdSense approval.

Significant Arguments, Facts, and Steps

1

Problem Identification

Many content creators struggle with "Low Value Content" rejections from Google AdSense after submitting 10-12 articles, often receiving consistent policy violation notices that their site "does not yet meet the criteria of use in the Google publisher network." Google's policy requires "unique high quality content" and "good user experience," explicitly warning against "thin content."

2

Speaker's Success

Achieved Google AdSense approval on the first submission, without any rejection emails, after learning WordPress, content writing, and SEO primarily from YouTube.

3

Content Length Requirements (Thin Articles/Blogs)

Advocates for creating substantial articles. The speaker's successful blogs ranged from 1200 to 1400 words. He explicitly notes that articles of 200, 300, 400, or even 800 words are considered "thin content." He created 12-15 such long-form blogs before submission.

4

Originality Over Rewriting/Copying

Strongly advises against directly copying content from any source, including AI tools like ChatGPT, or simply using "humanizer" tools to rephrase existing text. Content must be expressed in the writer's "own language."

5

Niche Expertise (No Expertise)

Highlights the importance of selecting a niche where one possesses genuine knowledge and interest. Lacking "expertise" in a topic prevents effective explanation and results in poor-quality content.

6AI

AI Content with Human Touch (Pro-Tip)

Proposes a "pro-tip" for those who struggle with writing: use AI tools (e.g., ChatGPT, DeepSeek) to generate initial content, but then significantly edit and enhance it with a "human touch." This manual refinement ensures the content feels authentically written by a human, improving its quality and AdSense eligibility.

7

Strategic Submission

Emphasized that websites should not be immediately submitted to AdSense right after publishing. He also worked on "basic layout" beyond just content.

Actionable Insights ("So What")

  • Prioritize Content Depth and Originality: To overcome "low-value content" rejections, consistently produce original, in-depth articles, targeting 1200-1400 words or more per post. This content must be genuinely created or substantially transformed by a human, not merely copied or spun.
  • Leverage AI Strategically, Not Solely: AI tools can serve as a starting point for content generation, but direct publication of AI-generated text without significant human editing and personalization is detrimental. Inject a "human touch" to elevate the content's quality, uniqueness, and adherence to Google's standards.
  • Build Authority Through Expertise: Focus on writing within niches where genuine interest and knowledge exist. This foundational expertise ensures the content is genuinely valuable, well-explained, and helps establish the site as an authoritative source.
