Fixing Google AdSense "Low Value Content" Errors

Your straightforward guide to getting your site approved by Google AdSense.

💡

The Problem: What is "Low Value Content"?

You've probably seen it: a Google AdSense policy violation saying your site has "Low Value Content." This means Google thinks your website isn't giving users enough helpful or unique information. It's like trying to sell a product that's not very interesting – nobody sticks around for long!

Our site, tattoosHowTo.com, got this warning. The main issue was that our content didn't meet Google's standards for its Publisher Network. We needed to make our pages more useful and original.

🛠️

Our Solution Path: More & Better Content

The fix isn't rocket science, but it requires effort. We had to focus on creating unique and high-quality content that truly helps people. Think of it as making a super-detailed guide instead of just a quick note.

Initially, we published 15 articles, hoping it would be enough. But we still had 10 more waiting in the drafts. Google wants to see a site that's packed with valuable information, so we knew we had to add more.

Key Steps We Took:

  • Keyword Research: We used a tool called SEMrush. It helped us find out what people are actually searching for related to tattoos and what questions they are asking (like the "People Also Ask" section on Google).
  • Content Generation: To speed things up, we used an AI article generator. Our goal was to create around 30 high-quality articles to show Google we were serious.
  • Automation Setup: Posting articles manually can be slow. So, we set up an automated system using a tool called Activepieces. This tool helps us post new content regularly without us having to do it every single time.
⚙️

Automating Content Publishing with Activepieces & WordPress

Activepieces is a super handy tool that connects different apps and automates tasks. Here’s how we used it to publish our AI-generated articles to our WordPress website:

  1. Scheduling: We used a Cron Expression in Activepieces. This is like setting a timer so that our automation runs at specific times, like posting 2 articles every day.
  2. WordPress API: We told Activepieces to talk to our WordPress site using its API (Application Programming Interface). Think of an API like a messenger that lets different software talk to each other.
  3. Publishing Posts: The specific command we used was to the WordPress API endpoint `/wp-json/wp/v2/posts`. We set it to `status=publish` and `per_page=1` to publish one new article at a time, making sure it went live correctly.

For this to work smoothly, we installed a WordPress plugin called "JSON Basic Authentication". This helps Activepieces securely log in to our WordPress site to post content.

⏳

Important Notes for AdSense Approval

There are a couple more things to keep in mind:

  • Site Age: Your website needs to be at least 30 days old before you can even submit it for an AdSense review. So, patience is key!
  • Resubmission: Once you've published enough high-quality content and feel your site is ready, you can resubmit it to Google AdSense for another look.
🚀

Actionable Insights: What You Should Do

So, what's the big takeaway? How can you avoid this "Low Value Content" issue or fix it if you already have it?

  • Always Create Great Content: Don't just write for the sake of it. Make sure your content is original, helpful, and directly answers what people are searching for.
  • Use Research Tools: Tools like SEMrush can show you what your audience wants. Use this info to create content that's in demand.
  • Embrace Automation: Tools like Activepieces and AI writers can help you create and post content much faster. This is great for scaling up your website.
  • Understand APIs: Learning how to connect your website with other tools using APIs can save you a lot of time and effort.
  • Follow Google's Rules: Always check the Google AdSense and Webmaster Quality Guidelines. Keeping up with their rules is super important.
  • Be Persistent: Fixing AdSense problems might take a few tries. Keep refining your content and strategy, and you'll eventually get approved.
